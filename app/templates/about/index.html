LionsMatter is a platform for the TCNJ community to publicly express concerns, comments, and suggestions. These posts can then be voted and commented on by other students, helping the College to better understand where its attention and priorities should be.

Instead of leaving posts to sit and fade into obscurity, LionsMatter works with TCNJ's Student Government to delegate posts to specific College departments. Departments are then given the opportunity to respond to each post, creating a new standard in transparency by showing how feedback is handled.

Account

Although anyone is welcome to visit the site and search through posts, only users are able to post, comment, or vote. LionsMatter attempts to verify user credentials through systems like TCNJ's CAS authentication while protecting users' privacy when it comes to making posts and comments.

TCNJ Unix Accounts

We participate in the same open standard that Canvas and PAWS use to sign you in; TCNJ authenticates your account for us so that LionsMatter will never see your password.

Anonymity is Important

You should have the right to post anonymously if you choose to. LionsMatter allows users to post anonymously if they wish, while also encouraging users to post free of anonymity.

Rules of the Conversation

There are certain expectations of decency between both students and the College. Certain basic rules such as no spam, profanity, offensive material, etc. are enforced through moderation.

Feedback

The mission of LionsMatter is to better connect The College of New Jersey with its students so that a free flow of information can occur. The site maintains a strong level of transparency by showing the route that every post takes from submission to response. The College faculty and staff are encouraged to respond to posts made by students to fuel this dialogue. LionsMatter provides the College with the benefit of a streamlined feedback system, while students benefit from actions that are motivated by their posts.

Post Status Updates

Users receive notifications as their post moves through the LionsMatter process. Posts are initially sorted by for the relevant College department, and then forwarded to a specific member within that department. Depending on the subject matter of the post, the staff member may submit a response to your post.

Responses

TCNJ department members will routinely respond to posts that are forwarded to them. The LionsMatter team works closely with departments to ensure posts are responded to in a timely manner, though some resolutions take more time than others.

Mediation

Once a post receives a response, the post's 'Comments' section will serve as a forum for the department member and users to discuss ongoing matters related to the post.

Posts

There are only three fields that users need to fill when submitting a post: content, category, and type. Users can post their questions, comments, and concerns in the content of the post. The post category allows the user to help in the sorting process by choosing where a post should be delegated. The post type allows the user to state whether their post is a comment, concern, or suggestion. Additionally, media submissions in the form of photos and Vines are accepted.

"Post as"

Each post or comment allows you to select the name that will be associated with it; either your full name, your username, or simply 'anonymous.'

Post History

Post history is a feature implemented to show the history of a post in the context of a conversation between the user who posted and the department that responded. By including post history a certain level of transparency is maintained and users can see that the College is actively engaged in this conversation.

Moderating

Although posts appear immediately, Student Government reserves the right to flag posts that contain obscene content or profanity; if flagged, a post is made un-viewable on the site until the user resolves the issue.
